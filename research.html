The Laws of Robotics.

There are 3 main laws of AI that all robots must follow.

A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.

A robot must obey the orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.

A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Laws.

Created by Isaac Asmiov

Types of AI

There are four different main types of AI.

Type One: Reactive Machines

These are the simplest machines, they do not have the capabilities to use past experiances to influence decisions, they are (hence the name) purely reactive. For example an early AI model that could play chess based of probabilty and single choice making was invented in 1990, it did not have much capability to take into account the future and effects of its decision. Reactive machines are a basic machine that is more Artificial than Intelligent when compared to the other types, it is not as smart or intelligent however very simple to create and implement therefore it is still classed as a relevant model for some projects and needs. They do not learn or adapt to future scenarios based of data and past experiances, they simply make a decision based of what is given to them.

Type Two: Limited Memory Machines

These are essentially the model that is a standard of what you would think modern AI can do. The Limited Memory Machine can observe, understand and interpret past experiances and data, a standard model type for this machine would be a Nueral Network, essentially an artificial equivalent of a brain. NNs(Nueral Networks) are a series of nuerons that act as nodes on a network of nuerons with different layers, all the nodes on each layer are interconnected to the next layer. Data is transposed and passed through the network, multiplied the bias or weight of the synapses in order to achieve a output. You can get different functions to output the data differently however the main concept to grasp is that the data is passed from layer to layer and an output is acheived. To start with the output will be a random guess as the weights of the synapses are not proper initialised as they are random. However by comparing the output to the expected ouptut of data / a past experiance the synaptic weights can be adjusted. Therefore after mass training and lots of adapatation this model can be extremely powerful, some self driving cars use this model and there are many other applications of it, for example the NHS use it to predict whether you are likely to get a certain illness like cancer based of multiple factors like heritage, chance and lifestyle.

Type Three: Theory of Mind Machines

This machine is very intelligent. Not only can in interpret and understand the world and data it is given, it can understand the entities in it, it can understand seperate emotions of people or animals. The name "Theory of Mind" is derived from psychology. Therefore the machine can understand why somethings act like it acts, not just notice that it acts in a pattern or way. It can understand why and therefore this machine is extremely intelligent and human like, because of this they can understand how society is formed and adapts. If the human race is ever to be coopeerable with AI then this will be the model that we socialise with, it will have to be able to understand how out society works and empathise with humans. Understanding emotions is critical to communications and if we are ever to work alongisde AI they would have to be able to understand how we feel.

Type Four: Self Aware Machines

This is an extension of TypeIII AI (Theory of Mind) and therefore maintains similar capabilities. However its one difference is a big one. Consciousness. It has the ability to be conscious. Therefore it is aware of it's existance, it knows it real, it knows that is has been made and therefore it has the capability to learn more than any other model. It's the "terminator" AI, the greatest yet most dangerous one. It's science fiction...for now. In 10 years time it may not be, as we make more and more progress towards greater models, it will slowly become reality and could be implemented. If you don't realise how important and colosoll being self aware is then just remember...
